English 101: College Composition & Analysis

  • Focus: Academic writing, critical reading, argumentation, and research skills.
  • Skills: Thesis development, evidence-based argument, MLA/APA citation, peer review, synthesis of sources.
  • Sample Assignments: Analytical essays, research papers, literary analysis, reflective writing.
  • Sample Texts: College-level essays, scholarly articles, classic and contemporary literature.

1. Academic Essay Structure

  • Introduction: Hook, background, thesis statement.
  • Body Paragraphs: Topic sentence, evidence, analysis, transitions.
  • Conclusion: Restate thesis, synthesize main points, closing thoughts.

2. Research & Citation

  • Find scholarly sources using databases (JSTOR, Google Scholar).
  • Integrate evidence with proper citation (MLA, APA, Chicago).
  • Avoid plagiarism by paraphrasing and quoting correctly.
  • Build annotated bibliographies and works cited pages.

3. Critical Reading & Analysis

  • Annotate texts for main ideas, arguments, and rhetorical strategies.
  • Analyze literary devices: metaphor, irony, symbolism, tone.
  • Evaluate arguments for logic, evidence, and bias.
  • Compare and contrast multiple texts and perspectives.

4. Revision & Peer Review

  • Draft, revise, and edit essays for clarity and coherence.
  • Participate in peer review: give and receive constructive feedback.
  • Use rubrics to assess writing quality and academic standards.

5. Study Tips

  • Attend office hours and writing workshops.
  • Use campus resources: writing center, library, tutoring.
  • Practice time management for reading, drafting, and research.
  • Read a variety of genres: essays, fiction, nonfiction, scholarly articles.

Tip: Practice writing short paragraphs and check grammar with online tools!

Study Guide

  • Practice writing outlines before essays. Learn how to structure introductions, body paragraphs, and conclusions. Study thesis statements, topic sentences, and transitions.
  • Review grammar rules and sentence types. Study parts of speech, subject-verb agreement, punctuation, clauses, and sentence variety. Practice editing for clarity and conciseness.
  • Use flashcards for vocabulary and literary terms. Expand to include figurative language, tone, mood, symbolism, irony, and rhetorical devices.
  • Read sample paragraphs and identify topic/support/conclusion. Analyze essays, articles, and literature for main ideas, supporting evidence, and argument structure.
  • Practice editing for punctuation and clarity. Learn proofreading strategies and common grammar mistakes.
  • Study reading comprehension strategies: skimming, scanning, annotating, summarizing, and inferring meaning. Practice with fiction, nonfiction, poetry, and drama.
  • Explore literary analysis: character development, plot structure, themes, and historical context. Write analytical essays and compare different works.
  • Learn research skills: finding credible sources, citing evidence, avoiding plagiarism, and synthesizing information.
  • Read classic and contemporary literature. Summarize key works, discuss themes, and practice critical thinking.
