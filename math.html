Introduction to Algebra

Algebra is the branch of mathematics that uses symbols and letters to represent numbers and quantities in formulas and equations. It is foundational for high school math, covering topics like linear equations, quadratic equations, polynomials, factoring, functions, and graphing. Algebra develops logical thinking and problem-solving skills essential for advanced math and science.

1. Solving Linear Equations

  • Equations of the form ax + b = c
  • Steps: Isolate the variable, simplify both sides, solve for x
  • Example: 3x + 7 = 16 → 3x = 9 → x = 3

2. Quadratic Equations

  • Equations of the form ax² + bx + c = 0
  • Methods: Factoring, completing the square, quadratic formula
  • Example: x² - 5x + 6 = 0 → (x-2)(x-3) = 0 → x = 2 or x = 3

3. Polynomials & Factoring

  • Polynomials: Expressions with multiple terms (e.g., 2x³ - 4x + 7)
  • Factoring: Breaking down polynomials into products of simpler expressions
  • Example: x² - 9 = (x+3)(x-3)

4. Functions & Graphs

  • Function notation: f(x) = x² + 2x
  • Graphing linear and quadratic functions
  • Identifying slope, intercepts, vertex, axis of symmetry

5. Algebra Vocabulary

  • Variable, coefficient, constant, exponent, term, expression, equation, solution
  • Function, domain, range, slope, intercept, quadratic, polynomial

Tip: Practice solving equations, graphing functions, and factoring polynomials. Use online tools like Desmos for visualization!

Study Guide

  • Review algebra steps for solving equations. Practice solving linear, quadratic, and systems of equations. Explore factoring, completing the square, and graphing solutions.
  • Practice geometry formulas (area, perimeter, volume). Study properties of triangles, circles, polygons, and 3D shapes. Learn proofs, theorems (Pythagorean, Triangle Inequality), and coordinate geometry.
  • Use flashcards for math vocabulary and symbols. Include terms like function, variable, coefficient, exponent, logarithm, matrix, vector, and calculus concepts.
  • Work through sample problems and check answers. Solve word problems, real-world applications, and challenge questions. Review step-by-step solutions and error analysis.
  • Explore graphing with online tools (Desmos). Learn about slope, intercepts, transformations, and curve fitting. Practice graphing polynomial, exponential, logarithmic, and trigonometric functions.
  • Study statistics: mean, median, mode, range, standard deviation, probability, and data interpretation. Practice creating and analyzing charts, histograms, and scatter plots.
  • Learn calculus basics: limits, derivatives, integrals, and their applications. Study tangent lines, area under curves, optimization, and motion problems.
  • Explore advanced topics: complex numbers, sequences and series, mathematical induction, and number theory.
  • Read math articles and watch educational videos. Summarize key concepts and practice explaining them to others.
